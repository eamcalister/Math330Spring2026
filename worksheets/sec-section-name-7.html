    The problem is to decide if \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n}\) converges.
    ðŸ”—
    This series is of the form \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-1)^{n+1}a_n\text{,}\) where \(a_n = \frac{1}{n}\text{.}\)

    
---

    Since \(\lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n} = 0\text{,}\) this series converges.
     #distractor
---

    
\(0 < \frac{1}{n+1} < \frac{1}{n} \text{,}\) i.e. the sequence of absolute values is positive and decreasing.
    
---

    \(\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{1}{n} = 0\)
    
---

    Thus, this series converges by the Alternating Series Test.
